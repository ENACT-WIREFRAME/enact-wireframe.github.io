National Diabetes Audit 2018-2019

Adults with type 2 diabetes whose HbA1c level is 58mmol/mol 7.5% or above after 6 months with single-drug treatment are offered dual therapy.

Results

Your practice achieved this standard of care for 86% (318/370) of patients.

The top 25% of practices achieved this for 93.7% or better of patients.

Recommendations for action

  • Identify adult patients with type 2 diabetes whose HbA1c level is 58mmol/mol or above.
  • Identify which of these patients have been treated with a single drug for 6 months or longer.
  • Invite these patients for a consultation to discuss HbA1c test results.
  • Discuss treatment options and offer dual-therapy, if appropriate.

For further information on target levels and treatment options please see NICE guidance

Patient Story#16

Daniel Kristeen

I had struggled to keep my diabetes under control for over a year. I was taking medication already and seriously trying to improve my diet. My Mum lost her sight because of diabetes and I was worried that this might happen to me. My doctor prescribed me another medicine and suggested I try it for a while to see if it made an improvement. Although I had doubts about starting another medicine, I’m glad that I did because my blood sugar is better now and I haven’t had any bad side effects.
