About Blood Transfusions

Patient blood management applies evidence-based medicine to transfusion practice, including the treatment and management of pre-operative anaemia, the management of haemostasis and blood conservation. Transfusion is a life-saving intervention in certain situations where no alternative exists, e.g. exsanguination or marrow failure. However, in most clinical settings, red cell transfusions are administered to patients without active bleeding. Randomised controlled trials indicate no evidence of benefit for policies of liberal transfusions and even suggest patient harm compared to restrictive use of blood. Patient blood management aims to make the most appropriate use of the limited donated blood supply whilst improving patient outcomes. Despite many national and international recommendations supporting patient blood management, previous audits and surveys have shown variable uptake across the UK and highlighted areas for improvement.
